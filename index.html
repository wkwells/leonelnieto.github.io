Message from Director

For many years, UDOT produced an annual Strategic Direction Report as a way of transparently reporting to the taxpayers of Utah, how we are investing funds allocated to us by the Utah State Legislature. We have taken advantage of the latest in online technology to provide a live, data and performance driven report that is constantly updated to reflect how we are reaching our strategic goals. The data we share here helps us make cost-effective decisions that help us meet the transportation challenges of today and the future. I hope that this live, dynamic report will be both interesting and useful as you continue to hold us accountable of our promise to Keep Utah Moving.

Carlos Braceras, P.E. Executive Director of UDOT

Connected and Autonomous Vehicles
Connected and Autonomous Vehicles

Utah is building the framework for a connected and autonomous vehicle (CAV) network in our state. CAVs will help us achieve our goal of Zero Fatalities by taking human error - which accounts for 94% of crashes - out of the equation. We are also uniquely situated to be a pioneer for CAVs because of our fiber backbone thanks to our approach with public-private partnerships.

In 2018, we became the first state to implement a fully functional connected vehicle deployment on Redwood Road. When a bus is running behind schedule, it “talks” to the traffic signal using dedicated short-range communications (DSRC) devices. The signal stays green just a little longer, and Utah Transit Authority buses can arrive on time in a very congested corridor. For more information on our CAV program, click here.

Unmanned Aerial Vehicles
Unmanned Aerial Vehicles

UDOT is looking ahead at the developing Vertical Take Off and Landing (VTOL) industry, how these new technologies could change the way we think about transportation, and we are preparing Utah to be ready for the first package delivery drones and aerial taxis. We envision 'highways in the sky' as seen in the image to the right, with smaller package delivery vehicles flying at altitudes between 400 and 600 feet above ground. We also envision larger aerial taxis utilizing the airspace between 600 and 800 feet above ground.

'Highways in the sky' is an appropriate analogy as we feel designing flight corridors over roads will help prevent noise pollution, protect citizens’ privacy by flying over designated corridors and not over homes, parks, or school, and an increased measure of safety as autonomous aerial taxis will have the same safety requirements held by the FAA as passenger airlines. For more information on our unmanned aircraft systems program, click here.

Incident Management Team
Incident Management Team

UDOT’s incident management program responder safety, reducing congestion and delays and reducing secondary crashes. Effective incident management is a team effort requiring vigilant monitoring and communication from the traffic operations control (TOC) center and swift action on the part of prepared field workers. Key roles IMT play at incidents include:

  • Working with Utah Highway Patrol and Fire to open
  • Assisting with cleanup debris and hazardous materials
  • Assisting stranded motorists

Since 2004, IMT has assisted more than 250,000 motorists while reducing secondary crashes and traffic delays on our roadways. Last year, we hired additional IMT responders increasing coverage hours by 86 percent to improve mobility and safety on our roadways.
